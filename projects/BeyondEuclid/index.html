Beyond Euclid

An Educational Virtual Reality Journey into Spherical Geometry.

UnityVRC#

Trailer: a quick look at spherical geometry fundamentals and basic map projections on a space journey.

About this project

This project was developed for a VR IEEE contest held in Florida. The competition’s theme was to create a VR educational application that explains a mathematical concept. Working in a team of three and supported by our professor, we chose to explore spherical geometry.

Because spherical geometry is inherently three-dimensional, VR offered an ideal medium to visualize its fundamentals—lines, triangles, and comparisons with Euclidean geometry. The experience takes the user on a journey inside a spacecraft, where they first learn about the core differences between the two geometries and then explore a real-world application: map projections. The user sees how different projection methods affect how the Earth is represented, making it clear why airplanes follow curved paths across maps.

What I learned

This project taught me the importance of team communication, clear task division, and writing code that others can easily read and extend. My role focused on the mathematical and algorithmic components—implementing spherical geometry visualization, 3D curved triangle meshes, and map projection logic. I also developed a VR interaction system where the user could draw geometric shapes on a virtual sphere positioned above their wrist using a stylus.

I contributed less to UI design, modeling, and puzzle-building, but the collaborative experience helped me understand how to merge different technical and creative workflows effectively.

The contest itself was an incredible experience. Each team presented their project at a booth where attendees could freely try the applications. Projects were evaluated on Mathematical Concept, Execution, VR Interaction, and Visual Appearance. Our project earned the Honorable Mention Award—a proud achievement that we brought back home to Europe as winners.
