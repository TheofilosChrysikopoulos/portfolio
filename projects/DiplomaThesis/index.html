About this project

This was my Diploma Project—an AR Digital Twin application for warehouse management. The goal was to solve the challenges of controlling a storage robot through software. The previous approach, based on a 2D floor plan, suffered from accuracy issues and lacked real-time awareness of the warehouse layout.

I addressed those limitations by building an AR application that provides precise spatial tracking and real-time visualization of the environment. Key objectives included accurate registration of the Digital Twin, efficient rendering of a detailed point-cloud model on a standalone Meta Quest 3 (with limited GPU resources), environment detection and meshing for virtual interactions, and a virtual storage robot capable of navigation and pathfinding in mixed reality.

Additional focus areas included realistic AR occlusion and shadow casting to blend virtual objects with the physical space, along with intuitive interaction methods and UI that support a smooth user experience.

What I learned

I deepened my expertise in Unity and XR development, creating advanced shaders to overcome hardware limits while maintaining visual quality. A major challenge was integrating multiple tools and libraries—resolving compatibility issues and navigating large codebases to implement targeted changes. I also learned to use LLMs effectively in my workflow—not to replace problem-solving, but to understand their strengths and weaknesses and work more productively.
